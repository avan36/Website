eQoScan

I worked on this Swift/SwiftUI app to reduce packaging waste on campus. When people want to check out a reusable food container, they first scan a location QR code and then their container's box code. They then return this container to get back a credit. I worked on coding the front-end of this app and integrating it with Google Authentication via FirebaseAuth and Firebase Real-time Database. I also implemented UserDefaults, AppStorage, Apple's location APIs for showing last scanned locations, and more. This was part of a group project, where I collaborated with others who delivered a backend server to generate QR codes.

We started this project at the WesHack Hackathon and it won the Internship Prize Track. We are continuing this project and trying to get Wesleyan University to implement this system.

I had a lot of fun presenting to Wesleyan's faculty on our research about student use of #GenerativeAI last Thursday with Rena Chen, Veronica Goss, and Sadie Cook! We've been working on this project as part of the Wesleyan Assessment Lab since September under the guidance of Rachael Barlow.

We surveyed 82 students from across 48 pop-up focus groups to learn how students are currently using AI in their academic/personal lives (e.g. content creation, revision of their writing) and why some students avoid it (e.g. fear of becoming reliant on it, skepticism about its abilities). Our findings reveal diverse student reactions to AI, and we're excited to work more in this area in the future!
