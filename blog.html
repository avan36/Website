The future of software

I've been wondering recently about the future of software as Generative AI has become part of our thinking about the workplace. It seems to me that, throughout software companies' history, their moat has been time and technical know-how...

For example, it would take many lifetimes to create the same software that Microsoft offers today, and it would require far more knowledge than any one individual could possess. Consequently, Microsoft has a moat around its products – they're hard and time-consuming to build.

"But here comes Generative AI, a new technology that can create virtually unlimited lines of code in no time..."

Of course, it would be impractical right now to, say, replicate a product like Microsoft Word using ChatGPT. How would you even go about that? For example, it's difficult in ChatGPT to handle multiple files, so you'd find yourself copying and pasting back and forth between the AI and the code editor. Of course, you could use a new IDE like Cursor, but even this has significant limitations on what you can build. Despite these current problems, it seems that someday (and we don't know when), AI will be both smart enough and cheap enough to build incredibly complex software all on its own.

I don't mean to wax hyperbolic or be yet another writer glorifying humanity's "AI future." Instead, I'm speaking from a practical perspective – this technology will get cheaper, faster, and better. And this brings me back to my point: what is the future of software if AI can produce any code and act like an agent?

I'm reminded of the famous example of GPT-4 producing an HTML/CSS website simply from an image. I've tried this myself, taking screenshots of various websites and asking ChatGPT to replicate them – the results were impressive. Soon (and by soon I mean anywhere from 1 to 10 years), I'll be able to take a screen recording of, say, the Duolingo app and get a working demo of it, complete with generated source code.

It seems one irreplaceable part of software will be the social features and network effects that have been built around them. For example, simply copying Facebook from a screenshot to create your own version would not be enough, as you wouldn't have all the content and the enormous friend graph from the network.
